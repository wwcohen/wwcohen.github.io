Introduction

I've always been interested in the mysteries around quantum mechanics (henceforth QM), but put off by the general perception that QM is somehow intrinsically magically obscure, and requires completely radical ways of thinking about things to make progress with. Certainly the math seemed very unfamiliar and hard (even though I do a lot of math for my day job!) and most of the prose I could find describing QM was more or less incomprehensible. In fact, Richard Feynman has a famous quote, "I think I can safely say that nobody understands quantum mechanics".

After spending sometime reading up on the area, I believe it's really not that bad---it's just that most QM material either tries to present all the math and all the specialized notation, or else tries to present the main ideas with no math at all. A better Feynman quote is from his lectures, below:

Because atomic behavior is so unlike ordinary experience, it is very difficult to get used to, and it appears peculiar and mysterious to everyoneâ€”both to the novice and to the experienced physicist. Even the experts do not understand it the way they would like to, and it is perfectly reasonable that they should not, ... we have to learn about them in a sort of abstract or imaginative fashion and not by connection with our direct experience.
Put another way: to explain QM, we can't use physical analogies, like we can with Newtonian physics, or electronics. We need to look at the math. I agree! But I disagree that we need to dive into hard math: we can get a reasonable understanding with math that a lot of people have. I'm coming to this area from AI/ML, and a lot of that background is quite useful, once you decode the QM notations.

This document is an introduction to QM using the math background that students of AI already have. In fact, all I will use is math that is part of a reasonably good AP high school education. While there will certainly be some math that may be unfamiliar to most students of AI/ML, it's generally things that were learned in high school or college and subsequently forgotten, because these things are not used much in modern AI. When this comes up I will try introduce the needed background gently. I will also replace QM notations with more familiar ones as much as I can.
